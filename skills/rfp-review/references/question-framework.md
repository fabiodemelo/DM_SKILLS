# RFP Analysis — The 30-Question C-Level Framework

Work through every question. Cite the RFP section/page for each answer. If the document does not answer it, mark **"Not specified — REQUEST CLARIFICATION"** and add to the Q&A list.

---

## Category 1 — Strategic Fit (4 questions)

**Q1. What is this RFP actually asking for?**
Summarize the scope in three sentences using plain language. Avoid copying the RFP's own wording — translate it.

**Q2. Does this align with our core competencies and 3-year strategy?**
Strong alignment, adjacent, or stretch? If stretch, is the stretch worth it?

**Q3. Is this a marquee logo, a door-opener to a new vertical, or just revenue?**
Name the strategic value beyond the immediate dollars.

**Q4. What is the follow-on revenue potential?**
Renewals, expansions, referrals within the buyer's org, pull-through product sales. Estimate with a range.

---

## Category 2 — Financial (5 questions)

**Q5. What is the Total Contract Value (TCV) and Annual Contract Value (ACV)?**
Cite the pricing schedule or budget range. If not disclosed, state "Budget not disclosed" and estimate from scope.

**Q6. What are the payment terms?**
Net-30/60/90? Milestone-based? Progress billing? Retainage held? Advance payment available?

**Q7. What is our expected gross margin?**
Estimate revenue minus direct cost of delivery. Flag if margin falls below the company's usual threshold.

**Q8. What is the cost-to-pursue (bid cost) vs. estimated win probability (Pwin)?**
Proposal hours, travel, sample work, bond costs, legal review. Pwin as a percentage with rationale.

**Q9. What is the cash flow impact?**
When do we spend vs. when do we get paid? Does this require working capital or financing?

---

## Category 3 — Resources & Capabilities (4 questions)

**Q10. How many people are needed, in what roles, at what seniority?**
FTE count by role (PM, engineer, designer, analyst, etc.) with start-to-end staffing profile.

**Q11. What is the initial investment needed before revenue starts?**
Tooling, hiring, certifications, facilities, equipment, software licenses. Dollar figure.

**Q12. Do we have the required past performance and references?**
Does the RFP demand specific past experience (e.g., "3 projects of similar scope in the last 5 years")? Can we meet it?

**Q13. What skill sets, clearances, or specialized capabilities are required that we don't currently have?**
Name them explicitly. Hire? Subcontract? Partner?

---

## Category 4 — Risk & Compliance (5 questions)

**Q14. What certifications, registrations, or clearances are required?**
ISO 9001/13485/27001, SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, PCI-DSS, FedRAMP, ITAR, CMMC, diversity certifications (MBE/WBE/DBE), state registrations, insurance minimums.

**Q15. What is the liability exposure?**
Liability cap (often a multiple of fees, sometimes unlimited), indemnification scope, consequential damages, IP infringement indemnity.

**Q16. What are the insurance and bonding requirements?**
General liability, professional liability (E&O), cyber, workers' comp, bid bond, performance bond, payment bond. Dollar amounts.

**Q17. What are the penalties, SLAs, and clawback provisions?**
Liquidated damages, service credits, termination-for-default triggers, warranty terms.

**Q18. What are the data security, privacy, and regulatory obligations?**
Data residency, breach notification timelines, subprocessor rules, audit rights, right-to-audit frequency.

---

## Category 5 — Commercial Terms (4 questions)

**Q19. What is the pricing model?**
Fixed price, Time & Materials, cost-plus, milestone, per-unit, subscription, outcome-based. Implications for risk.

**Q20. Who owns the IP?**
Work-for-hire (buyer owns all)? We retain background IP? Joint ownership? Source code escrow required?

**Q21. Can we subcontract, and under what terms?**
Restrictions on subcontractor use, required disclosure, flowdown clauses, approval rights.

**Q22. What are the termination rights?**
Termination for convenience (with what notice, what fees)? Termination for default (what cure period)? Survival clauses?

---

## Category 6 — Competitive Landscape (3 questions)

**Q23. Is there an incumbent? Is this a re-compete or greenfield?**
Incumbents typically win 70-80% of re-competes unless the buyer is actively looking to switch. Named in the RFP or inferred.

**Q24. Who are the likely competitors, and what is our differentiation?**
List 3-5 likely bidders. State our unique selling points that map to the RFP's evaluation criteria.

**Q25. What is our estimated probability of winning (Pwin)?**
Use a framework: relationship (0-25%), past performance fit (0-25%), technical fit (0-25%), price competitiveness (0-25%). Sum to a percentage.

---

## Category 7 — Timeline & Process (3 questions)

**Q26. What are the key dates?**
Tabulate: RFP issue date, Q&A deadline, site visit / pre-bid conference, proposal due date, award date, contract start, contract end, renewal option dates.

**Q27. Are there required meetings, site visits, or oral presentations?**
Pre-bid conference (mandatory vs. optional), site visits, oral presentations with panel, demos. Logistics and cost.

**Q28. What is the evaluation and award process?**
Scoring rubric (price vs. technical weighting), evaluation panel composition, best-value vs. lowest-price, negotiation rounds expected, BAFO (Best And Final Offer) round?

---

## Category 8 — Go/No-Go Decision (2 questions)

**Q29. Bid, No-Bid, or Conditional-Bid?**
State the recommendation in one word, then the rationale in one paragraph. If Conditional-Bid, list the conditions (e.g., "only if we partner with X", "only if liability cap is negotiated below $Y").

**Q30. What are the top 3 questions we should submit to the issuing authority during Q&A?**
Strategic Q&A submissions — questions that will change our bid strategy, not nitpicks.

---

## Note on Adaptation

Not every RFP will answer every question. The goal is not to force-fit an answer but to surface the gaps. A brief that says **"10 of 30 questions unanswered — recommend No-Bid until clarification obtained"** is more valuable than a brief that invents answers.
